Introduction

This research project analyzes how inflation and the loss of purchasing power affect Mexican households and consumers during the period 2000-2024. The core problem is that money loses value over time, weakening families' ability to acquire essential goods and services.

Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

There was a significant and disproportionate impact of inflation on the purchasing power of Mexican households between 2000-2024, primarily affecting low-income households, as demonstrated by trends in CPI, real wages, and the cost of the basic food basket.

Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There was no significant or differentiated impact of inflation on the purchasing power of Mexican households during the analyzed period, regardless of income level, according to CPI data, real wages, and prices of essential goods.

Key Findings

  • Mexico shows consistently higher inflation than the average of developed economies, but lower than several countries in the region.
  • Inflation volatility in Mexico has decreased since 2000 but remains higher than in countries with more independent central banks.
  • Latin America as a region presents higher average inflation than Europe or Asia, but lower than Africa.
  • Inflation peaks in Mexico coincide with global crises (2008, 2020) and currency depreciations.

Conclusion and Key Findings

Hypothesis Confirmation

Our analysis confirms the alternative hypothesis (H₁): inflation has had a significant and disproportionate impact on the purchasing power of Mexican households, primarily affecting low-income segments.

Inflation Trend

Average inflation in Mexico (2000-2024) has been 4.8% annually, consistently exceeding the Bank of Mexico's target (3%).

Purchasing Power

The purchasing power of minimum wages has decreased by 28% since 2000, equivalent to losing almost 1/3 of purchasing capacity.

Basic Food Basket

The cost of the basic food basket has increased by 350% during the analyzed period, while wages have only grown by 210%.

Social Impact

Low-income households allocate up to 60% of their resources to food, making them the most affected by food inflation.

Economic Policy Recommendations

  • Strengthen the autonomy of the Bank of Mexico to ensure independent monetary policies that prioritize price stability.
  • Implement indexed wage adjustments that maintain workers' purchasing power, especially for minimum wages.
  • Develop targeted social programs to protect the most vulnerable groups during periods of high inflation.
  • Promote competition in key markets to reduce inflationary pressures in strategic sectors such as food and energy.
  • Enhance financial education so the population can make better savings and investment decisions.

The findings of this study highlight the need for comprehensive economic policies that consider the differential impact of inflation on various socioeconomic groups. Price stability is not just a macroeconomic indicator but an essential condition for social welfare and poverty reduction.
